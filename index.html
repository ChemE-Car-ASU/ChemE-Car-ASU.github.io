Learn More

The American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) sponsors regional and national Chem-E Car competitions where College students design and build a chemically powered vehicle that is able to safely travel a specified distance and stop

We are a multidisciplinary team. AIChE is a professional organization of chemical engineers, but our club values the strengths and talents of other engineering disciplines. By working together and allowing others to contribute time and skill, we can accomplish a more complex and sophisticated design. We know that our best chance of producing a winning car is through hard work and collaboration.

Meet the Team

Structure Team

Structure Team: Responsible for designing the chassis of the car that will safely carry necessary components, including reactors, electronics, sensors, etc.

Skills Required
  • Solidworks/CAD experienc
  • Arduino programming sensors
  • Research materials
  • Hands-on experience in assembly
Join the structural team

Reaction Team

Determine the reactions used to start and stop the car, as well as determining conditions and sensors needed for the processes.

Skills Required
  • Chemistry knowledge
  • Experimentation in Lab
  • Data analysis and tracking
  • Research alternative reactions for future iterations
Join the reaction team

Research Team

Mainly responsible for writing and submitting proposals to ensure eligibility for future competitions in addition to leading the search for reactions that can be used in future Chem-E Car designs.

Skills Required
  • Writing / Filling out required paperwork
  • Collecting members safety trainings
  • Ensure team abides by AIChE rules, safety, and trainings
  • Research alternative reaction mechanisms
  • Research lighter, stronger, more resistant, etc. materials
Join the research team

Rectruitment Sub-Team

Encourage and invite current students to join Chem-E Car through in-person presentations, club outreach, social media and a website. Additional responsibilities include tracking budget and working directly with sponsors and the university.

Skills Required
  • Good Communication
  • Writing emails
  • Reaching out to professors/spinsers
  • Website Building
  • Social Media
Join the rectruitment team
